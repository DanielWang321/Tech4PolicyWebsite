Turn Your Ideas Into Impact

A civic-tech competition empowering Allegheny County students to solve local challenges with technology and policy.

Our Mission

Bridging the Gap Between Code and Community.

"Tech4Policy" is a competition by a Nonprofit organization where student teams build a plan. Each Team submits two connected components:

  • A Tech-Based Solution: A prototype/draft of an app, website, or tool that solves a local problem.
  • A Policy Brief: A one-page implementation plan on how the local government could realistically adopt, fund, and support the solution.

Competition Timeline

Registration Opens

Assemble your team and register for the competition. We will provide resources and guides to help you get started.

Solution Development Period

Teams will develop their tech prototype and draft the accompanying one-page policy brief. Examples and scoring guidelines will be provided.

Submissions & Judging

All projects are due by March 31. Our preliminary judges will review all submissions based on our public rubric, narrowing the field to the top 5 finalists. Finalists will be announced on April 15th.

The Finale & Winners Showcase

The top 6 teams will present their projects live to our final judging panel, including Rep. Venkat. Winners will be announced, and all finalists will recieve certificates. Winners would recieve prize money
